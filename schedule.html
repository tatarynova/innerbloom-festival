Theme: Where light meets sound.

The Horizon Stage is an open-air amphitheater framed by warm, golden lighting and large LED panels that create slow-evolving visual landscapes. Designed for live melodic and emotional electronic performances, it blends organic visuals with modern scenography. Soft sand flooring and tiered viewing areas give the space a relaxed, sunset-friendly atmosphere. Surrounding food courts and lounge zones let visitors drift easily between dancing, socializing and enjoying the music. This stage is ideal for the warm vocal-driven sets of Jan Blomqvist and Monolink, building into ARTBAT’s powerful late-night energy.

Theme: A nature-inspired sonic sanctuary.

This small organic space is modeled after a forest clearing, decorated with lanterns, low-hanging fairy lights, and illuminated sculptures. Beanbags, hammocks, and small fire pits create pockets of relaxation away from the main stages. Here, ambient DJs and local downtempo artists play soothing sets until late at night. It’s a place to decompress, reconnect with friends, or recharge between performances. Herbal tea bars and quiet zones reinforce the calm, making the Garden a peaceful end-of-night retreat.

Theme: Lights that move with the night.

The Aurora Stage is the festival’s largest nighttime arena, known for its immersive lighting architecture inspired by the Northern Lights. High-tech laser arrays, towering LED pillars, and 180° projection mapping wrap the audience in flowing colors and glitch-infused motion. The sound system is optimized for deep bass and crisp melodic elements, making it perfect for Lane 8’s long journey sets, RÜFÜS DU SOL’s cinematic live performance, and Boris Brejcha’s signature high-tech minimal sound. As darkness falls, the entire stage visually transforms — shifting from sunset pastels into intense neon waves.
